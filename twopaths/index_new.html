Status Update: Why the Proposed Amendment Was Not Filed

Proposed Amendment - EMC Transparency and Accountability

A comprehensive consumer-protection amendment was drafted to address the most dangerous consequences of repealing Florida's Personal Injury Protection (PIP) system.

  • Independent Emergency Medical Condition (EMC) determinations
  • Actual medical examinations (in-person or telemedicine - not paper reviews)
  • Prohibition on insurer-controlled or paper-only peer reviews
  • Clear medical necessity standards
  • Cost controls without shifting power to trial lawyers
Despite being fully drafted and ready, the legislative filing deadline passed before the amendment could be formally introduced.

As a result:

  • The public was denied a transparent, consumer-focused alternative
  • Lawmakers are now being asked to vote on PIP repeal without safeguards
  • Florida drivers are being exposed to higher litigation risk and higher premiums

This website now serves as a public explanation and accountability record of what was proposed - and what was ignored.

The Reality: There Are Still Two Paths

Florida lawmakers had - and still have - two choices:

Path One: Fix PIP

  • Preserve no-fault coverage
  • Eliminate abuse through independent medical review
  • Require real exams, not paperwork
  • Reduce litigation incentives
  • Protect access to care

Path Two: Repeal PIP - But Only With Consumer Protections

  • Independent medical decision-making
  • No insurer-controlled exams
  • No paper-only reviews
  • Clear reimbursement limits tied to medical necessity
  • Guardrails against litigation abuse

What Florida drivers are being offered now is neither.
